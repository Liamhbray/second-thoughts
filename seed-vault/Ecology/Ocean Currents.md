# Ocean Currents

Ocean currents are continuous movements of seawater driven by wind, temperature, salinity, and the Earth's rotation. They are the circulatory system of the planet, distributing heat, nutrients, and marine life across ocean basins.

## Relevance to Whales

Currents shape whale ecology in multiple ways:

### Upwelling Zones
Where deep, nutrient-rich water rises to the surface (driven by wind and Coriolis forces), it fuels phytoplankton blooms that sustain the food web. These are critical feeding areas for [[Baleen Whales]], concentrating [[Krill and Prey Ecology|krill]] into dense patches.

The California Current, Humboldt Current, and Antarctic Circumpolar Current are among the most productive whale habitats on Earth.

### Migration Corridors
[[Whale Migration]] routes often follow current boundaries where temperature gradients provide navigational cues. Warm currents flowing poleward and cold currents flowing equatorward create predictable pathways.

### Sound Propagation
The SOFAR channel — a layer of minimum sound speed created by temperature and pressure gradients — allows whale calls to travel thousands of kilometres. [[Whale Communication]] depends on this acoustic waveguide, which is shaped by current-driven temperature structure.

## Climate Change

Warming oceans are altering current patterns, shifting upwelling zones, and changing prey distributions. This forces whales to adapt their migration routes and feeding strategies, with uncertain consequences for population recovery. See [[Threats to Whales]].
