# Krill and Prey Ecology

Antarctic krill (*Euphausia superba*) are the keystone prey species for [[Baleen Whales]]. These small crustaceans (up to 6 cm) form swarms so dense they are visible from space, with biomass estimated at 300-500 million tonnes — one of the most abundant animal species on Earth.

## Krill Biology

Krill feed on phytoplankton under sea ice and in upwelling zones driven by [[Ocean Currents]]. They have a 5-7 year lifespan and can shrink their body size during food scarcity — an unusual metabolic adaptation.

## Whale-Krill Relationship

The relationship is a feedback system. Whales consume vast quantities of krill (a single [[Blue Whales|blue whale]] eats 4 tonnes/day), but whale faeces fertilise the phytoplankton that krill eat. This is the [[Whale Pump]] in action — whales actively sustain the productivity of their own food source.

Before industrial whaling removed 2 million whales from the Southern Ocean, this nutrient cycle was far more productive. Paradoxically, krill populations have *declined* since whales were removed — likely because the [[Whale Pump]] no longer fertilises enough phytoplankton.

## Other Prey

Not all whales depend on krill:
- [[Sperm Whales]] hunt squid at depth
- Gray whales sift amphipods from seafloor sediment
- [[Humpback Whales]] also target herring and sand lance

## Threats

Krill populations are declining due to warming seas and loss of sea ice. Commercial krill fishing (for aquaculture feed and omega-3 supplements) adds additional pressure. See [[Threats to Whales]].
