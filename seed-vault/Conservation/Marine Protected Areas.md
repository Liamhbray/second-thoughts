# Marine Protected Areas

Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) are designated ocean regions where human activity is restricted to protect marine ecosystems. For whales, they serve as refuges from ship strikes, fishing entanglement, and noise pollution.

## Types

- **No-take zones**: all extraction prohibited — strongest protection
- **Multi-use MPAs**: some activities allowed (e.g., sustainable fishing) with restrictions
- **Whale sanctuaries**: IWC-designated areas where commercial whaling is permanently banned (Southern Ocean Sanctuary, Indian Ocean Sanctuary)

## Key Whale MPAs

### Stellwagen Bank (USA)
Critical feeding ground for [[Humpback Whales]], fin whales, and right whales. Ship speed restrictions reduce strike mortality.

### Pelagos Sanctuary (Mediterranean)
Protects sperm whales, fin whales, and dolphins in a heavily trafficked sea.

### Ross Sea MPA (Antarctica)
The world's largest MPA. Protects critical feeding habitat for [[Blue Whales]] and minke whales, where [[Krill and Prey Ecology|krill]] concentrations are highest.

## Effectiveness

MPAs work best when they cover entire [[Whale Migration]] corridors, not just endpoints. A whale that is protected on its breeding ground but exposed to ship strikes on its migration route receives incomplete protection.

Dynamic MPAs — areas that shift in real time based on whale sighting data — are an emerging approach that balances conservation with shipping efficiency.

## Limitations

Only ~8% of the ocean is in MPAs, and most lack enforcement. Climate-driven shifts in [[Ocean Currents]] are moving whale habitat outside existing MPA boundaries. See [[Threats to Whales]].
