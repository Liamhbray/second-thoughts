# Blue Whales

The blue whale (*Balaenoptera musculus*) is the largest animal known to have existed. Adults reach 30 metres and 180 tonnes — their heart alone is the size of a small car.

## Biology

Despite their size, blue whales feed almost exclusively on krill (see [[Krill and Prey Ecology]]). A single adult consumes up to 4 tonnes of krill per day during feeding season, using the lunge-feeding technique described in [[Baleen Whales]].

Their tongues weigh as much as an elephant. Their blood vessels are wide enough for a human to swim through. A blue whale calf gains roughly 90 kg per day on its mother's milk.

## Vocalisation

Blue whales produce the lowest-frequency sounds of any animal — deep pulses and moans between 10-40 Hz that can travel thousands of kilometres through the ocean. These are part of the broader [[Whale Communication]] system, likely used for long-range contact and mate attraction.

## Population

Pre-whaling populations were estimated at 200,000-300,000. By the 1960s, fewer than 10,000 remained. Current estimates suggest partial recovery to 10,000-25,000 worldwide — a direct consequence of [[Whaling History]] and subsequent [[Marine Protected Areas]].

Blue whales undertake vast seasonal journeys described in [[Whale Migration]].
