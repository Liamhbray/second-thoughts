# Sperm Whales

The sperm whale (*Physeter macrocephalus*) is the largest toothed predator on Earth. Males reach 18 metres and 57 tonnes. Their heads constitute up to one-third of their body length, housing the spermaceti organ — a massive reservoir of waxy oil whose function is still debated.

## Deep Diving

Sperm whales are the deepest-diving mammals, routinely reaching 1,000-2,000 metres and holding their breath for up to 90 minutes. At depth, they hunt giant and colossal squid in total darkness using echolocation (see [[Toothed Whales]] and [[Whale Diving]]).

Their myoglobin-rich muscles store extraordinary amounts of oxygen. Ribs are flexibly attached, allowing the chest cavity to compress under extreme pressure.

## Social Structure

Females and calves live in stable matrilineal groups of 10-20 individuals. Males leave the group as adolescents and become increasingly solitary, visiting female groups only to mate. Groups use distinct patterns of clicks called codas — regional dialects that function as cultural identity markers (see [[Whale Communication]]).

## The Whale Fall

When a sperm whale dies and sinks, its massive body creates a [[Whale Falls|whale fall]] — a deep-sea ecosystem that can sustain specialised communities for decades.

## Historical Significance

Sperm whales were the primary target of the 19th-century whaling industry due to their spermaceti oil and ambergris. See [[Whaling History]].
