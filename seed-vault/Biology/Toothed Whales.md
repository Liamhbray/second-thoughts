# Toothed Whales

Toothed whales (Odontoceti) are the larger of the two cetacean suborders by species count. They hunt individual prey using echolocation — a biological sonar system unmatched in the animal kingdom.

## Echolocation

Toothed whales produce high-frequency clicks in the nasal passages, focus them through the melon (a fatty organ in the forehead), and receive echoes through the lower jaw. This allows them to detect prey in total darkness at depths exceeding 2,000 metres.

[[Sperm Whales]] produce the loudest biological sound ever recorded (230 dB), used for both echolocation and communication.

## Key Species

- **[[Sperm Whales]]**: the largest toothed predator, diving to 2,250 m to hunt giant squid
- **Orcas**: apex predators with complex social structures and culturally transmitted hunting techniques
- **Beaked whales**: deep-diving specialists, among the least known large mammals on Earth
- **Dolphins and porpoises**: the most diverse group, found in every ocean

## Social Structure

Many toothed whales live in stable social groups. Orca pods maintain distinct dialects passed between generations — a form of culture. [[Whale Communication]] in toothed species ranges from echolocation clicks to complex social calls.
