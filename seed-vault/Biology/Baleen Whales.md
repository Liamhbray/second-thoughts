# Baleen Whales

Baleen whales (Mysticeti) are the largest animals ever to have lived. Instead of teeth, they filter-feed using plates of baleen — flexible keratin structures that hang from the upper jaw like a comb.

## Feeding Strategies

Baleen whales use three main feeding techniques:

- **Lunge feeding**: the whale accelerates into a dense prey patch and engulfs enormous volumes of water, then pushes it out through the baleen. [[Blue Whales]] and [[Humpback Whales]] use this method.
- **Skim feeding**: the whale swims slowly with its mouth open, continuously filtering. Right whales specialise in this.
- **Bubble netting**: a cooperative technique unique to [[Humpback Whales]], where a group blows a rising spiral of bubbles to corral prey before lunging upward.

## Key Species

The suborder includes [[Blue Whales]], [[Humpback Whales]], gray whales, minke whales, and right whales. They range from the 8-metre minke to the 30-metre blue whale.

## Evolutionary Note

Baleen and [[Toothed Whales]] diverged roughly 36 million years ago. The evolution of baleen is linked to the expansion of Antarctic krill populations driven by [[Ocean Currents]] and continental drift.
